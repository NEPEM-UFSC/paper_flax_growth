About

Research group, authors, and funding information.

Research group

This work was developed at the NEPEM – Núcleo de Estudos em Pesquisa e Experimentação com Milho (Study Group on Research and Experimentation), within the Department of Plant Science (Departamento de Fitotecnia) of the Federal University of Santa Catarina (UFSC), Florianópolis, Brazil.

Study location

Experiments were conducted at the Centro de Ciências Agrárias (CCA/UFSC), Florianópolis, Santa Catarina, Brazil (27°35’S, 48°31’W; 2 m a.s.l.). The subtropical climate of the region is classified as Cfa (Köppen) — humid subtropical with hot summers and mild winters — making it an important testbed for cool-season crops such as flax.

Citation

Authors. (2025). Logistic model reveals early sowing as key to maximizing flax (Linum usitatissimum L.) vegetative growth under subtropical conditions. Agronomy Journal. doi: pending

Reproducibility

All analyses were performed in R (R Core Team, 2024) using a fully reproducible Quarto workflow. Source code and data are publicly available at:

🔗 github.com/nepem-ufsc/paper_flax_growth

Key R packages used:

Package Purpose Reference
metan Multi-environment trial analysis, doo(), corr_plot() Olivoto & Lúcio (2020)
AgroR Agricultural ANOVA, FAT2DBC() Olivoto et al. (2022)
broom Tidy NLS output Robinson et al. (2023)
hydroGOF Goodness-of-fit metrics (R², RMSE) Zambrano-Bigiarini (2023)
ggplot2 Data visualisation Wickham (2016)
patchwork Multi-panel figures Pedersen (2024)
ggridges Ridge density plots Wilke (2024)

Contact

For questions or collaborations, please open an Issue on GitHub.

All analyses were performed in **R** (R Core Team, 2024) using a fully
reproducible Quarto workflow. Source code and data are publicly available at:

🔗 [github.com/nepem-ufsc/paper_flax_growth](https://github.com/nepem-ufsc/paper_flax_growth)

|---------|---------|-----------|
